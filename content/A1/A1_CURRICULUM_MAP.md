# Habla A1 Curriculum Map

Version 1.0 — Course production plan

## Purpose

This is the at-a-glance production map for Habla A1. It prevents grammar duplication, vocabulary gaps, story contradictions, disconnected missions, and missing rewards.

- `HABLA_CANON.md` remains the authority for story facts.
- `HABLA_TEACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md` remains the authority for teaching decisions.
- `content/A1/lesson-03-family.json` remains the production structure reference.
- Stable content IDs remain unchanged so routing and saved progress stay safe, even when a learner-facing title replaces an older working title.
- A planned lesson is not production-ready until it passes `content/LESSON_QA_CHECKLIST.md`.

#### Chapter 1 continuity

Chapter 1 repeatedly returns to three anchors: Mercado de San Miguel, Plaza Mayor, and Carlos and Marta’s apartment. Each return serves a new communication goal so Madrid becomes familiar rather than decorative.

| Lesson | Naturally recycles | Story handoff |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | No prior language | Carlos offers to introduce Ana. |
| 2 | Greeting, name, `mucho gusto`, check-in, closing | Carlos invites Ana and the learner to dinner. |
| 3 | Greeting, names, origin, residence, closing | Carlos suggests meeting again. |
| 4 | Family dinner, family names, greetings, numbers already heard | Carlos hints that he needs something special at the market. |
| 5 | Day, time, family needs, planned meeting | The mystery is resolved; after the learner chooses and buys an item, Carlos suggests a coffee break. |
| 6 | Market items, quantities, prices, greeting a new person | Carlos proposes a walk through Madrid. |
| 7 | Café location, time, polite repair phrases | Rain begins and Carlos invites everyone home. |
| 8 | Places, location, family members, shopping items | Carlos describes what he does at home each day. |
| 9 | Home, rooms, time, family, everyday objects | Carlos proposes one full weekend adventure. |
| 10 | All Chapter 1 communication outcomes | The family invites the learner to Granada. |

| 11–14 | Weather chunks, adjective agreement, question words, profession identity | Returns when choosing activities and describing people or work. |

| 21–28 | Route commands, `ir a`, polite help, `necesito`, phone chunks, `poder`, reservation chunk, `para` | Returns in connected travel missions. |
| 29–30 | Review and integration only | No new grammar. |

### Spiral rule

A later lesson may reuse an earlier form without reteaching it. If a lesson expands an earlier form, it must name the new communicative use. For example:

- Lesson 7 teaches `está` for locating places; Lesson 19 teaches `estar + feeling`, which is a new communicative use.
- Lesson 1 teaches `¿puede repetir?` as one survival chunk; Lesson 23 expands `¿puede + infinitive?` for requesting help.
- Lesson 5 teaches numbers through prices; Lessons 22, 26, and 28 reuse them for tickets, money, and departure information.

## Production order

1. Lessons 1–3 — aligned and validated.
2. Lessons 4–10 — produce the complete Madrid mini-season.
3. Chapter 1 continuity and learner testing.
4. Lessons 11–20 — produce Granada only after Chapter 1 passes.
5. Chapter 2 continuity and learner testing.
6. Lessons 21–30 — produce Valencia and the A1 final challenge.

No lesson enters production only because its content file exists. The map defines intent; the QA checklist defines completion.
