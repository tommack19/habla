# Habla A1 — One Journey, Thirty Conversations

## Promise

After Lesson 30, a learner should be able to hold a supported 5–10 minute conversation in slow, natural Spanish and manage the most common beginner situations without memorizing a script.

Habla A1 is the MVP. A2, B1, and B2 remain out of scope until this journey is complete, coherent, and tested.

The detailed production map for all lesson titles, story events, grammar targets, missions, rewards, review hooks, and handoffs is [A1_CURRICULUM_MAP.md](./A1_CURRICULUM_MAP.md).

## Teaching principles

The governing product principles are defined in `HABLA_TEACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md`. Story continuity is frozen in `HABLA_CANON.md`. Lesson approval requires both `content/CONTENT_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md` and `content/LESSON_QA_CHECKLIST.md`.

1. Teach situations before word lists.
2. Prefer reusable Tier 1 language over topic trivia.
3. Introduce one small grammar pattern per lesson.
4. Recycle earlier language in every new conversation.
5. Let Carlos narrate the journey and close every lesson with a real mission.
6. Keep explanations short enough to use on a phone.
7. Never claim the learner can do something the lesson has not practiced.

## The lesson rhythm

Every lesson follows the same ten-part rhythm:

1. Carlos Introduction — what is happening and what the learner will do.
2. Vocabulary — 15–30 high-frequency words grouped by purpose.
3. Essential Phrases — natural chunks that solve the situation.
4. One Grammar Pattern — explained through meaning, not terminology.
5. Listening — identify meaning from short, level-appropriate audio.
6. Speaking — hear, repeat, and produce the target phrases.
7. Mini Conversation — a guided exchange with Carlos.
8. Cultural Note — one useful paragraph, then move on.
9. Carlos Challenge — complete one realistic mission.
10. Review — quiz, flashcards, and a short conversation recap.

## Vocabulary tiers

- Tier 1 — needed constantly; the core of Habla A1.
- Tier 2 — useful every week; included when the situation requires it.
- Tier 3 — occasional; normally deferred or taught as an optional note.
- Tier 4 — nice to know; excluded from core A1 lessons.

## Chapter 1 — Madrid: Build Your Foundation

The learner meets Carlos and spends a connected first day in Madrid.

| Lesson | Situation | Communication outcome | One grammar pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Meet Carlos | Greet someone, exchange names, and close naturally | `me llamo` and `soy` for identity |
| 2 | Meet Ana | Say where you are from, where you live, and ask the same | `soy de` vs `vivo en` |
| 3 | Meet Carlos’s Family | Identify close family and describe relationships | `mi`, `tu`, and `su` |
| 4 | See You at Ten | Arrange a market visit using a day and a simple meeting time | days and simple time expressions |
| 5 | Fresh Fruit and Fresh Spanish | Help Carlos choose and buy ingredients for lunch | `este`, `esta`, `estos`, `estas` |
| 6 | Coffee Break | Order a drink and snack and make one polite change | `quisiera` |
| 7 | Walking Through Madrid | Ask where places are and follow a short route | `está` for location |
| 8 | An Afternoon at Home | Name rooms and say what exists and where it is | `hay` vs `está` |
| 9 | A Typical Day | Describe four parts of a normal day | first-person regular present |
| 10 | Weekend Adventure | Complete a connected Madrid mission using Lessons 1–9 | Chapter 1 consolidation |

**Passport destination unlocked:** Madrid

## Chapter 2 — Granada: Live Your Everyday Life

The learner moves from survival phrases into short personal conversations.

| Lesson | Situation | Communication outcome | One grammar pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| 11 | Talk About the Weather | Describe today’s weather and choose a simple activity | weather with `hace`, `está`, and `hay` |
| 12 | Talk About Work | Say where and when you work and ask about someone’s job | regular present tense in context |
| 13 | Learn Something New | Ask for repetition, clarification, and help understanding | question words |
| 14 | Say How You Feel | Describe common symptoms and ask for basic help | `tener` expressions |
| 15 | Take the Bus or Metro | Ask which route to take and where to get off | `tener que` for necessity |
| 16 | Eat at a Restaurant | Order a full meal, respond to questions, and ask for the bill | polite requests with `puede` |
| 17 | Make a Phone Call | Answer, ask for someone, and leave a short message | `¿Está…?` and `dígale…` as chunks |
| 18 | Invite Someone Out | Invite, accept, decline, and suggest another time | `¿Quieres…?` |
| 19 | Talk About Your Weekend | Say what you like and describe simple activities | `me gusta` + infinitive |
| 20 | Make Weekend Plans | Arrange a short plan from invitation to goodbye | near future with `ir a` |

**Passport destination unlocked:** Granada

## Chapter 3 — Valencia: Travel With Confidence

The learner applies the language independently across one connected trip.

| Lesson | Situation | Communication outcome | One grammar pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| 21 | Check Into a Hotel | Confirm a reservation and ask about essential services | `tengo una reserva` |
| 22 | Navigate the Airport | Find check-in, security, a gate, and boarding information | destination questions |
| 23 | Take a Train | Buy a ticket and confirm platform, destination, and time | `para` for destination |
| 24 | Visit a Museum | Buy entry, ask opening times, and understand basic rules | `se puede / no se puede` as chunks |
| 25 | Shop at a Market | Ask for quantities, prices, and recommendations | quantities with `un poco de` |
| 26 | Visit a Doctor | Explain where it hurts and understand simple instructions | `me duele / me duelen` |
| 27 | Handle an Emergency | State the problem, location, and help needed | urgent commands as fixed phrases |
| 28 | Make Small Talk | Start, sustain, and close a friendly conversation | conversation connectors |
| 29 | Valencia Review | Complete a connected hotel-to-dinner travel day | Chapter 3 consolidation |
| 30 | Your First Full Spanish Journey | Complete a multi-scene mission with Carlos | A1 integration, no new grammar |

**Passport destination unlocked:** Valencia

## A1 exit outcomes

A learner who completes the final mission can:

- introduce themselves and ask basic personal questions;
- talk simply about family, work, routines, interests, and plans;
- understand the main idea of slow, clear speech in familiar situations;
- order food and coffee, shop, and ask for prices;
- ask for and follow basic directions;
- manage simple hotel, airport, train, health, and emergency interactions;
- repair a conversation by asking for repetition or slower speech;
- sustain a supported 5–10 minute conversation with Carlos.

## Content production rule

Lessons are rewritten one at a time. A lesson is not considered finished until its Spanish is reviewed, every answer is valid, its challenge uses previously introduced language, and the lesson can be completed end to end in the app.
