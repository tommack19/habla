# Habla Design Philosophy

## Product vision

Habla is a story-driven Spanish learning experience where the learner builds confidence through real situations, cultural moments, and an ongoing relationship with Carlos.

The app should feel like living through a journey in Spain, not completing isolated textbook exercises.

## Core principles

1. Every lesson tells a continuous story.
2. Spanish is always introduced in context.
3. A1 learners should never be left guessing what an important prompt means.
4. New phrases should appear in meaningful dialogue before they are tested.
5. The learner should understand why each activity exists.
6. Carlos is a supportive friend and guide, not a strict examiner.
7. Learner choices should affect the story whenever practical.
8. The app should remember the learner’s name, location, preferences, and past choices.
9. Culture should feel lived-in and personal, not like a detached fact box.
10. Progress should feel like collecting experiences, memories, stamps, and confidence—not only XP.
11. Practice should coach the learner toward what will help most next.
12. Every screen should move the learner closer to communicating confidently in Spanish.

## A1 accessibility rules

- Show English beneath important Spanish instructions and story-choice questions.
- Keep Spanish visually primary and English clearly secondary.
- Avoid testing vocabulary or grammar before it has been introduced.
- Use believable distractors in quizzes, not unrelated answers.
- Break difficult speaking tasks into small guided steps.
- Prefer short, natural instructions over long explanations.
- Never display internal labels such as “Learner” when the user’s name is available.

## Story and character rules

- Maintain character identities, relationships, locations, and chronology across lessons.
- Carlos should reference previous events and remembered choices naturally.
- Dialogue should sound like something real people would say.
- Recurring characters should use consistent voices.
- Story transitions should create anticipation for the next lesson.
- Completion screens should feel like chapter endings, not score summaries.

## UI principles

- One clear primary action per screen.
- Avoid redundant sections and repeated explanations.
- Prevent clipping, overlap, awkward wrapping, and hidden content on small phones.
- Navigate to the top whenever a new lesson section opens.
- Keep hero text concise.
- Use artwork to support the story without obscuring text or cropping the subject badly.
- Keep Home, Learn, Practice, Carlos, and Me visually related but functionally distinct.

## Feature identities

- Home: current story, journey progress, and the best next action.
- Learn: structured curriculum and story episodes.
- Practice: adaptive coaching and targeted skill improvement.
- Carlos: live conversation, feedback, and personal tutoring.
- Me: profile, passport, travel journal, achievements, and long-term progress.

## Definition of done

A feature or lesson is not complete until:

- content is valid,
- progression works,
- mobile layout is verified,
- audio works,
- accessibility is reviewed,
- story continuity passes,
- Spanish sounds natural,
- English support is sufficient for A1,
- and the experience matches this philosophy.
