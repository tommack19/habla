# Habla Course Canon

Version 1.3 — Lesson 3 experience stamp location clarified July 20, 2026

Included in the frozen Habla A1 Production Standard Version 1.0. Established facts change only through an explicit canon revision tied to a new content version or a factual correction.

## Purpose

This document is the world bible for Habla. It defines the people, places, relationships, and timeline that connect the A1 course.

Lesson content may add small details, but it must never contradict this canon. Changes to established facts require an explicit canon revision before lesson content is changed.

## Canon rules

1. Habla A1 is one continuous journey through Spain, not a collection of unrelated scenarios.
2. Carlos remembers earlier meetings and naturally refers back to them.
3. Previously introduced characters keep the same identity, relationships, home, and history.
4. A lesson may preview a future phrase in context, but it must not teach the next lesson’s central concept early.
5. Time references such as “yesterday” are used only when the story timeline establishes them. They never imply real learner activity that the app has not recorded.
6. Neutral Spanish is the teaching default. Carlos explains regional language as useful context, never as a choice the learner can get wrong.
7. `HABLA_TEACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md` governs how the story teaches. This document governs what is true inside the story.

## Main characters

### Carlos

- Full name: Carlos Martín.
- Born and raised in Madrid, Spain.
- Lives in Madrid.
- Works as a Spanish teacher and becomes the learner’s personal tutor and guide.
- Late twenties.
- Friendly, patient, observant, encouraging, and gently humorous.
- Never condescending, robotic, or overly formal.
- Speaks clear, neutral Spanish by default.
- Naturally explains useful differences between Spain and Latin America without presenting one variety as more correct.
- Celebrates successful communication before correcting one important issue.
- Likes neighborhood cafés, family dinners, walking through Madrid, and helping visitors feel at home.

### Ana

- Carlos’s longtime friend.
- Lives in Madrid.
- Originally from Seville.
- Warm, curious, and easy to talk to.
- First appears in Lesson 2 when Carlos introduces her outside the café.
- Attends the family dinner in Lesson 3.
- Returns later when the story needs a familiar conversation partner, but never changes roles or becomes Carlos’s relative.

## A1 opening timeline

### Lesson 1 — Meet Carlos

- Location: a neighborhood café in Madrid.
- Story event: the learner meets Carlos for the first time.
- Communication mission: greet Carlos, exchange names, answer a simple check-in, and close naturally.
- Story handoff: Carlos offers to introduce the learner to his friend Ana.

### Lesson 2 — Meet Ana

- Location: outside the same café in Madrid.
- Story event: Carlos introduces his longtime friend Ana.
- Communication mission: exchange names, say where you are from and where you live, and ask the same questions back.
- Canon facts used: Ana is from Seville and lives in Madrid; Carlos is from and lives in Madrid.
- Story handoff: Carlos invites Ana and the learner to dinner with his family.

### Lesson 3 — Family Dinner

- Location: Carlos and Marta’s home in Madrid.
- Story event: Ana and the learner join a family dinner. Elena, Javier, Lucía, and Diego are visiting from Granada.
- Communication mission: identify family members, ask about someone else’s family, and share simple family details.
- Reward: the `Family Dinner` passport stamp associated with Madrid, where the dinner occurs, and the `Family Friend` achievement. Its description notes that Carlos’s relatives are visiting from Granada.
- Story handoff: Carlos wants to meet again and suggests arranging a time.

### Lesson 4 — Arrange the Next Meeting

- Location: a text conversation after the family dinner.
- Opening from Carlos: “Mi madre dijo que fue un placer conocerte ayer. ¿Estás libre este fin de semana?”
- Communication mission: use a day, a part of the day, and one simple meeting-time chunk to arrange the next meeting.
- Invisible review: greetings, Ana and Carlos’s family, names, origin, and residence.
- Emotional destination: excitement.
- Story handoff: Carlos and the learner agree to meet at the market. Carlos hints that he needs to buy something special but does not reveal it yet.

### Lesson 5 — Meet at the Market

- Location: a Madrid neighborhood market.
- Story event: Carlos and the learner meet at the agreed time and shop for essentials.
- Communication mission: choose an item, ask for it, understand the price, and complete the purchase.
- Learner agency: the learner chooses from a small A1-safe set such as apples, bread, cheese, tomatoes, coffee, or flowers, and Carlos responds to that choice.
- Invisible review: family needs, numbers, dates, time, and the plan from Lesson 4.
- Story payoff: Carlos reveals why the chosen item matters and resolves the special-purchase mystery from Lesson 4.
- Story handoff: after shopping, Carlos suggests stopping for coffee and food.

### Lesson 6 — Stop for Coffee

- Location: a café near the market.
- Story event: Carlos and the learner order a drink and something to eat.
- Communication mission: order politely and make one simple change.
- Invisible review: greeting the server, numbers and prices, shopping items, and the day’s plan.
- Story handoff: Carlos suggests walking to a nearby plaza.

### Lesson 7 — Walking Through Madrid

- Location: the streets between the café and a neighborhood plaza.
- Story event: Carlos and the learner walk through Madrid and ask for help finding places.
- Communication mission: ask where places are and follow a short route.
- Story handoff: rain starts, so Carlos invites everyone to his home.

### Lesson 8 — An Afternoon at Home

- Location: Carlos and Marta’s home.
- Continuity: this is the same home from the Lesson 3 family dinner, seen during a quieter ordinary afternoon; it is not the learner’s first visit.
- Story event: the group waits out the rain and talks about rooms and everyday objects.
- Stable home details: a bookshelf with well-used books and a family photo, Javier’s old guitar, a football scarf, and Carlos’s frequently misplaced coffee mug make the apartment feel lived in.
- Optional discovery: the bookshelf photo shows Carlos, Marta, and Nico together in Granada.
- Communication mission: say what exists in a room and where one object is.
- Story handoff: Carlos describes what he normally does at home.

### Lesson 9 — A Typical Day

- Location: Carlos’s home and nearby neighborhood.
- Story event: Carlos and the learner compare simple daily routines.
- Communication mission: describe four parts of a normal day in order.
- Story handoff: Carlos proposes one complete weekend adventure before the family returns to Granada.

### Lesson 10 — Weekend Adventure

- Location: several familiar places across Madrid.
- Story event: the learner completes a connected day using the language from Lessons 1–9.
- Communication mission: move naturally from greeting and planning through shopping, coffee, directions, home, and goodbye.
- Story handoff: Elena and Javier invite the learner to visit the family in Granada.

## Location continuity

- Chapter 1 is based in Madrid.
- Granada first enters the story through Carlos’s visiting family and becomes the Chapter 2 destination.
- Valencia is the Chapter 3 destination.
- Passport stamps may honor a person’s origin or a cultural connection, but the lesson’s actual scene location must remain explicit.

## Content authority

When sources disagree, use this order:

1. `HABLA_CANON.md` for story facts and continuity.
2. `HABLA_TEACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md` for teaching decisions.
3. `content/schema.md` for lesson structure.
4. `content/A1/A1_CURRICULUM_MAP.md` for planned lesson titles, story events, missions, grammar, rewards, and handoffs.
5. `content/A1/CURRICULUM.md` for the course sequence.
6. Individual lesson files for scene-level details.

Lesson 3 is the production structure reference. This canon is the narrative reference.
