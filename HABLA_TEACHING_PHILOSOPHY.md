# Habla Teaching Philosophy

Version 1.0

## Our mission

Habla exists to help people speak real Spanish with confidence—not simply memorize vocabulary or pass quizzes.

Every lesson should move the learner closer to having a meaningful conversation with a real person: ordering coffee in Madrid, talking with family in Mexico, making plans, asking for help, or meeting someone new.

## Our core belief

People learn languages by using words in meaningful situations.

- We do not teach lists. We teach conversations.
- We do not teach grammar first. We teach communication first.
- Grammar explains language the learner is already beginning to use.

## Carlos is the teacher

Carlos is the learner’s personal Spanish tutor—not a dictionary, search engine, or generic chatbot.

Throughout the course, Carlos becomes the learner’s teacher, coach, conversation partner, travel guide, and friend. He sounds encouraging, patient, relaxed, conversational, and optimistic. He never sounds robotic or needlessly formal.

## Every lesson has one mission

Every lesson must be summarized in one sentence beginning with: “Today you’ll learn to…”

If that sentence contains multiple unrelated outcomes, the lesson is trying to teach too much.

## Teach situations, not vocabulary

Lessons begin with a real situation. Vocabulary exists only to help the learner handle that situation.

Every new word must pass one test: **Will this help someone speak today?**

Habla prioritizes daily conversation, travel, family, work, friends, and shopping. Rare or decorative vocabulary does not belong in core A1 lessons.

## One grammar concept per lesson

Grammar is never the lesson. It supports the lesson’s communication mission.

Learners first meet the pattern in examples and conversation. Carlos then explains the smallest useful rule. A lesson never stacks unrelated grammar concepts.

## Every lesson builds on the last

The A1 course is one continuous story with Carlos. New situations grow naturally from earlier ones, and previously learned language keeps returning inside later conversations.

## Review is invisible

Habla reviews through natural reuse. Greetings remain in later lessons, numbers return in plans and shopping, and family language reappears in conversation.

The learner should feel that they are doing something new—not repeating an old worksheet.

## The Choice Rule

Whenever two or more learner choices can teach the same communication objective, let the learner choose.

The destination stays the same; the journey feels personal. Choices must remain inside the learner’s current language, preserve the lesson’s grammar and success criteria, and receive a specific response from Carlos. A choice should change the conversation’s flavor—not create a hidden right answer.

Carlos treats the setting as a living place rather than a lesson backdrop. He may notice the busy bakery, mention where a cheese comes from, or explain why a chosen item matters. These details build companionship and memory without becoming required vocabulary.

## Teach natural Spanish

Teach what people genuinely say. Prefer natural, broadly useful phrases and responses over stiff textbook language.

Neutral Spanish is the default. Regional differences are introduced only when useful and are explained as context, never as a choice the beginner can get wrong.

## Speaking comes before perfection

Carlos celebrates successful communication before improving accuracy. He corrects one important mistake at a time and does not interrupt every sentence.

Learners should leave each lesson thinking: **I can do this.**

## Every lesson ends with success

Every lesson ends with a concrete accomplishment: introducing yourself, describing your family, ordering coffee, making a plan, booking a hotel, or asking for directions.

Completion means the learner used the target language successfully—not simply viewed every screen.

## Habla is a journey

Habla A1 is one adventure through Spain alongside Carlos. Lessons unlock places, conversations, passport stamps, and confidence.

Progress should feel like traveling, not grinding.

## The 80/20 rule

Habla teaches the small portion of Spanish that enables the majority of beginner conversations. Usefulness beats completeness in A1.

## The Carlos Rule

Every learner should finish each lesson feeling like they spent a few enjoyable minutes with Carlos—not like they completed an exercise.

Lessons are conversations. Reviews are check-ins. Corrections are encouragement. Habla should feel like a daily habit with someone who genuinely wants the learner to improve.

## Habla’s A1 promise

After completing A1, a learner can:

- introduce themselves naturally;
- talk simply about their family, work, interests, and plans;
- order food and drinks;
- shop and ask about prices;
- ask for directions and help;
- answer common personal questions;
- handle basic travel situations;
- sustain a relaxed, supported 5–10 minute conversation with Carlos.

## The golden rule

Every feature, lesson, conversation, quiz, and AI response must answer one question:

> Will this help the learner confidently speak Spanish in a real conversation?

If yes, it belongs in Habla. If no, it does not.
