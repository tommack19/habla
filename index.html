Your 12-Month Roadmap

From zero to confident conversational Spanish

1
Survival Spanish
Months 1–2 · A1 Beginner
Don't worry about being perfect — build the skeleton first.
  • Week 1–2: Greetings, numbers 1–100, days & months
  • Week 3–4: Ser vs Estar (to be), subject pronouns
  • Week 5–6: Present tense regular verbs (-ar / -er / -ir)
  • Week 7–8: Colors, family vocab, simple sentences

Daily habit: 15 min with Carlos + 10 min Duolingo. Target: 300 words.
2
Building Real Sentences
Months 3–4 · A2 Elementary
Start forming your own sentences and asking questions.
  • Irregular verbs: tener, ir, querer, poder, hacer
  • Past tense: Preterite (pretérito indefinido)
  • Questions: ¿Dónde? ¿Cuándo? ¿Quién? ¿Cómo?
  • Practical vocab: Shopping, directions, restaurants, travel

Add: Easy Spanish on YouTube — 10 min/day of real street conversations.
3
Hold a Conversation
Months 5–7 · B1 Intermediate
The hardest phase — push through and real conversations become possible.
  • Imperfect tense — describing the past (era, tenía, vivía)
  • Future tense — plans and predictions (voy a, iré)
  • Reflexive verbs — levantarse, llamarse, sentirse
  • Subjunctive intro — quiero que…, espero que…
  • Opinion phrases — creo que, me parece, en mi opinión

Add: Find a language exchange partner on Tandem app — 2× 20-min calls/week.
4
Think in Spanish
Months 8–12 · B2 Upper Intermediate
Consume Spanish media naturally. Your accent solidifies here.
  • Podcasts: Español con Juan, Coffee Break Spanish
  • TV: La Casa de Papel, Club de Cuervos (Netflix) with Spanish subtitles
  • Reading: Children's books → news articles → novels
  • Advanced grammar: Subjunctive mastery, conditional tense

Goal: 30-minute unscripted conversation with your wife's family by Month 12.
💡 The #1 Secret to FluencyConsistency beats intensity every time. 20 minutes every day beats a 3-hour weekend session. Use Carlos daily — even a 5-minute check-in keeps Spanish active in your brain.
🎯 Why This Works for YouLearning Spanish to connect with your wife's family is one of the most powerful motivators there is. When you know your "why", you push through the tough weeks. Keep that image in mind — the look on their faces when you speak Spanish to them.
